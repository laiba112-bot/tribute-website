A.P.G Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man Of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, often hailed as the "Missile Man of India," was a distinguished aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. His contributions to India's space and missile programs, including the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles, were pivotal in advancing the country's technological capabilities. Kalam's vision extended beyond science; he was also a passionate advocate for education and youth empowerment. Biography

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
  • Came from a modest family; his father was a boat owner and his mother was a homemaker.
  • Studied physics at St. Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli.
  • Earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Worked at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • Joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), contributing to satellite launch technology.
